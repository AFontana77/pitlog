# PitLog — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Pit masters, weekend BBQ enthusiasts, smoker hobbyists. Phone in apron pocket or on a wooden picnic table next to the smoker. They're checking cook times for brisket, looking up the right temp for pork shoulder, logging cooks so next time turns out the same. Beer nearby. Smoke in the air.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **smoky, weathered, masculine.**

This isn't a recipe blog. This is a working reference for people who've been smoking meat for 5+ years. Charred, rust-stained, low-and-slow. Not a startup, not a foodie magazine.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark theme.** A smoker at dusk. Embers glowing. The site should feel like the inside of a smokehouse, not a Whole Foods website.

**Font pair:**
- Display: `Alfa Slab One` — heavy slab serif, BBQ menu / wood-burned signage feel. Heavy and confident. NOT in reflex list.
- Body: `Asap` — slightly condensed humanist sans, utilitarian, holds up at small sizes on dark backgrounds.

**Color system (OKLCH, dark warm):**
- Background: `oklch(0.13 0.025 50)` — dark smoky warm (not blue-black)
- Surface: `oklch(0.18 0.030 50)` — slightly lighter dark warm
- Foreground: `oklch(0.93 0.020 50)` — warm cream
- Muted foreground: `oklch(0.62 0.018 50)`
- Border: `oklch(0.28 0.025 50)`
- Primary (copper/amber): `oklch(0.62 0.16 55)` — copper/ember
- Primary light: `oklch(0.72 0.14 55)`
- Accent (smoke red): `oklch(0.55 0.18 30)` — for highlighted callouts

### Design Principles
1. **Cook times and temps as the design element.** "203°F internal" is more important than any feature copy.
2. **Heavy-weight type creates atmosphere** — Alfa Slab is the smoky-signage feel.
3. **No icon cards.** Use tables of cuts × times × temps.
4. **Editorial dark layout, not dashboard dark** — nothing glowing-cyan-on-black tech-bro stuff.
5. **Respect the user's expertise.** They know what "stall" means. Don't over-explain.
